GCP Polynomial Order

The degree of fit in the georeference process defines the degree of the mathematical polynomial equation used to calculate the latitude/longitude, UTM, or MGRS coordinate grid. The accuracy of the resulting grid may be greater with a higher degree of fit.

A higher degree of fit requires more input GCPs, so the minimum number of GCPs changes based on the selected degree of fit. This number is displayed in the Georeference dialog for reference. The root mean square (RMS) error gives an indication of the relative accuracy for each GCP.

Opticks does not allow you to select a degree of fit higher than 3.
